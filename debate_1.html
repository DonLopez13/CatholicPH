Introduction

The debate between the authority of Scripture alone (sola scriptura) and the authority of the Church is a fundamental issue in Christianity. The Catholic Church teaches that both Sacred Scripture and Sacred Tradition, with the Church as its interpreter, are essential sources of divine revelation. In contrast, many Protestant denominations uphold sola scriptura, asserting that the Bible alone is the final authority.

Catholic Viewpoint

The Catholic Church teaches that the Church has the authority to interpret Scripture, as the Church itself was established by Christ to safeguard and teach His teachings.

"Sacred Scripture is the word of God, but it is the Church that has the authority to interpret and preserve its truth." - St. Thomas Aquinas

Protestant Viewpoint

Proponents of sola scriptura argue that the Bible itself claims to be the final authority in matters of faith and practice, pointing to passages such as 2 Timothy 3:16.
