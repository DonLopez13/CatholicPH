Introduction

The debate over salvation by faith alone (sola fide) or by faith and works is a major theological divide. Protestants emphasize salvation by faith alone, while Catholics believe that faith must be accompanied by works.

Catholic Viewpoint

The Catholic Church teaches that faith is essential for salvation, but it must be expressed through good works, which include following the commandments and performing acts of charity.

"Faith without works is dead; works without faith are dead." - St. Thomas Aquinas

Protestant Viewpoint

Protestants generally believe that faith alone, not works, justifies a person before God, citing passages like Ephesians 2:8-9.

Conclusion

The Catholic Church teaches that salvation involves both faith and works, which complement each other in the journey of salvation.
