Introduction

The authority of the Pope is a point of contention between Catholics and many Protestant denominations. Catholics believe the Pope is the spiritual successor of St. Peter, endowed with supreme authority over the Church.

Catholic Viewpoint

The Catholic Church teaches that Christ appointed Peter as the rock upon which the Church would be built, and that the Pope inherits this authority.

"You are Peter, and upon this rock I will build my Church." - Jesus Christ (Matthew 16:18)

Protestant Viewpoint

Many Protestant groups reject the notion of papal supremacy, viewing the authority of the Pope as unnecessary and unscriptural.
