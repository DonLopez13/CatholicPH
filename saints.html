St. Thomas Aquinas

St. Thomas Aquinas (1225–1274) was one of the greatest theologians and philosophers of the Catholic Church. He is known for his work *Summa Theologica*, where he combined reason with faith. He played a crucial role in developing Scholasticism and defending the Church against heretical ideas. His teachings on natural law, morality, and theology continue to influence Catholic doctrine today.

St. Augustine of Hippo

St. Augustine (354–430) was a brilliant philosopher and theologian. His works, including *Confessions* and *The City of God*, shaped Christian thought for centuries. A former sinner turned saint, Augustine’s teachings on grace, free will, and the nature of evil are still studied today.

St. Francis of Assisi

St. Francis (1181–1226) was the founder of the Franciscan Order. Known for his love of nature and devotion to poverty, he inspired millions with his simple yet profound faith. He famously received the stigmata (wounds of Christ) and preached to all of creation, even animals.
