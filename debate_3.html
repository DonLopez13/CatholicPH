Introduction

The Catholic Church holds that Mary is the Mother of God (Theotokos) and plays a unique role in the economy of salvation. Protestants, however, often see her as a faithful servant but not deserving of any special reverence beyond that of any other human woman.

Catholic Viewpoint

The Catholic Church teaches that Mary, as the Mother of Jesus, who is God, holds the title of Theotokos. This acknowledges her unique role in salvation history.

"Mary is the Mother of the Head, and the Head is Christ." - St. Thomas Aquinas

Protestant Viewpoint

Protestants believe that while Mary is honored as the mother of Jesus, she should not be venerated or called the Mother of God in the same way Catholics do.
