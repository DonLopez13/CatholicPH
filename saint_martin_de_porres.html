Saint Martin de Porres - Patron of Social Justice and the Poor

Saint Martin de Porres (1579-1639) was born in Lima, Peru, to a Spanish nobleman and a freed African slave. Despite the prejudice and discrimination he faced due to his mixed heritage, Martin's life was one of profound humility, charity, and service to others. He became a member of the Dominican Order, where he served as a lay brother, tending to the sick and caring for the poor.

Saint Martin was known for his deep spirituality, humility, and compassion. He practiced extraordinary works of charity, often serving the poor and sick in the slums of Lima. Martin was also a healer and had the gift of bilocation, where he was said to appear in two places at once, and miracles of healing were attributed to his intercession.

He is the patron saint of social justice, the poor, and mixed-race people. Saint Martin's canonization was an acknowledgment of his holiness, as he led a life of virtue despite the challenges he faced due to his race and status.
